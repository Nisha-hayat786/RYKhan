Rahim Yar Khan

Introduction:

Rahim Yar khan is the city of the province Punjab ,district Rahim Yar khan . It was known as "Naushehra" until 1881. So , the ruler of Bahawalpur, Nawab Sadiq Khan renamed it after his first son, Rahim Yar Khan . District Rahim Yar Khan lies between 27.40′ – 29.16′ north latitudes and 60.45′ – 70.01′ east longitudes of about 11,880 square kilometers. The district comprises four Tehsils namely Rahim Yar Khan, Sadiqabad, Liaquat Pur and Khan Pur.In Rahim Yar Khan raees, Makhdoom, Bajwa, Arain, Jut, Rana, families are well known political families. Mostly there were Punjabi speaking families of arain cast.

Agriculture:

Agriculture is the back bone of Pakistan and Rahim Yar Khan has an agri-based economy with 65% of people living here have agriculture as their core occupation known for its high production of cotton, sugarcane, rice, and wheat crops.

Bhong Mosque:

It is one of the most attractive and historical mosque of Pakistan located in the village of Bhong ,Rahim Yar Khan , Punjab. It was designed and constructed with architectural beauty and the stylish calligraphic work and it won the Aga Khan Award for Architecture in 1986. It is a public place and people can visit whenever they want .

Adam sahaba:

It is the place about which it was said that there the two companions of Holy Prophet Hazrat Muhammad P.B.U.H are buried. Adam Sahaba is 8 km far from Rahim Yar Khan city .

Patan Minara:

It is an ancient tower (lighthouse), situated eight kilometers from Rahim Yar Khan. It was said that this area was demolished by the 18th century and they discovered a brick with sanskrit written on it.
